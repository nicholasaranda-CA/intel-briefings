Topic Area 1
U.S. Foreign Policy
4 stories
Breaking
Operation Epic Fury: U.S. and Israel Launch Massive Strike on Iran, Killing Supreme Leader Khamenei
On February 28, 2026, U.S. and Israeli forces launched approximately 900 strikes in just 12 hours against Iranian missiles, air defenses, military infrastructure, and senior leadership—the opening of what the U.S. designated Operation Epic Fury. The coordinated assault killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of top Iranian officials in the first wave. The strikes also struck a girls' school adjacent to an IRGC naval base near Bandar Abbas, killing more than 160 civilians; Israel denied responsibility and the U.S. said it would investigate. Trump framed the operation as aimed at preventing Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons and suggested a four-week timetable.

"Iran had hidden highly enriched uranium in an underground facility undamaged by previous strikes — the IAEA said it had no evidence of an organized nuclear weapons program, but could not be sure."

— Britannica, March 2026
Britannica / Roll Call / CNN Feb–Mar 2026
Analysis
Foreign Aid Gutted: USAID Shuttered, $9B+ in Rescissions
The Trump administration shut down USAID and slashed foreign assistance from $38.4 billion in FY2025 to an estimated $8.1 billion for FY2026 after rescissions. CFR estimates more than 350,000 deaths have been attributed to the cuts since January 2025. European allies, including France, Germany, and the UK, made additional parallel cuts, compounding the humanitarian toll.
Council on Foreign Relations Dec 2025
Diplomacy
New START Expires; U.S.–Russia Nuclear Framework Collapses
The last remaining arms control treaty between Washington and Moscow expired on February 5, 2026, ending decades of strategic stability verification. The U.S. and Russia together hold 87 percent of the world's nuclear warheads, with no successor agreement in sight.
CFR / Stimson Center Feb 2026
Americas
U.S. Forces Capture Maduro in Venezuela; Western Hemisphere Operations Expand
In a January 3, 2026, operation involving 150 aircraft, U.S. forces captured Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro and his wife. Separately, a Caribbean Sea and Eastern Pacific campaign (Sep 2025–Jan 2026) targeted drug traffickers, deploying the USS Gerald Ford. In total, U.S. forces conducted 493 strikes in Trump's second term.
EU Institute for Security Studies / CFR Jan 2026

U.S. Politics
4 stories
Executive Power
DOGE Winds Down After $20B in Cuts — Far Short of $2T Target
Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency effectively concluded its largest phase with a final tally of roughly $20–40 billion in annual savings — a fraction of the $2 trillion Musk initially claimed. Congress largely rejected the administration's discretionary spending cut proposals in the FY2026 budget, with only 1 of 30 sampled programs eliminated. Budget experts now expect battles to shift to courts, where judges will decide the legality of Trump's impoundments and agency personnel reductions.
Washington Times / Brookings Institution Feb 2026
Immigration
Courts vs. Executive: Third-Country Deportations Become Constitutional Flashpoint
The Supreme Court allowed the Trump administration to resume swift deportations to third countries (June 2025), while a Massachusetts federal judge ruled in February 2026 that such removals without notice or due process are unlawful. The administration deported individuals to South Sudan despite State Department warnings about conditions there. The Court's 2025 term saw it side with the administration on the Alien Enemies Act, termination of Temporary Protected Status, and universal injunction authority — the administration filed as many emergency Supreme Court requests in 20 weeks as Biden did in four years.
Marshall Project / Al Jazeera / NBC News Jun 2025 – Feb 2026
2026 Midterms
Medicaid Cuts Emerge as Defining Battleground for 2026 Elections
Democrats have declared the Republican "One Big Beautiful Bill Act's" $700–880 billion in projected Medicaid cuts the "defining contrast" of the 2026 cycle. The DCCC is running a "Tax Scam" messaging campaign targeting vulnerable House Republicans. The law also inadvertently triggers $45 billion in automatic Medicare sequestration cuts for 2026 under the Statutory PAYGO Act — growing to $536 billion over nine years — though Congress could waive the sequestration. Senate Republicans are reportedly uncomfortable with the scope of cuts.
NBC News / PBS NewsHour / CBO Jul–Aug 2025
War Powers
Congress Debates War Powers Resolutions on Iran; Passage Unlikely
Both chambers are considering resolutions that would bar additional Iran military action without congressional authorization. Speaker Johnson acknowledged supplemental defense funding discussions are underway, but passage of a binding war powers check is considered unlikely given Republican leadership alignment with the administration's Iran posture.
Roll Call Mar 3, 2026
Judiciary
Supreme Court's IEEPA Tariff Ruling Looms as Major Economic Wildcard
The Supreme Court is evaluating the constitutionality of Trump's tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act. A ruling against the administration could force refunds of approximately $180 billion in annualized tariff revenue and require the administration to find alternative legal authority — creating significant near-term economic volatility.
SIEPR Stanford / J.P. Morgan Jan 2026

U.S. Economy
5 stories
Tariffs
Tariff Regime at Highest Rate Since 1932 — Average Household Faces $1,500 Hit
The Trump tariffs have elevated the average effective U.S. tariff rate to 16.9% — the highest level since 1932 — before accounting for new Greenland-related tariffs that could push that figure to 17.5%. The Tax Foundation estimates this constitutes the largest U.S. tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993, imposing an average burden of $1,500 per household in 2026. Roughly 40–50% of tariff costs have been passed on to consumers so far, with businesses expected to pass through more as pre-tariff inventory depletes. Yale's Budget Lab projects a 0.4-point drag on GDP growth and 1.3 million fewer payroll jobs by year-end.

"The negative effects of tariffs on GDP growth have yet to play out fully — the impact on consumption won't be fully seen until consumer prices rise further."

— Morningstar, January 2026
Tax Foundation / Yale Budget Lab / Morningstar Jan 2026

Labor Market
Job Growth Slows to 50K/Month; Stagflation Risk Rises
Monthly payroll growth has slowed from approximately 110,000 in 2024 to just 50,000 in 2025, with unemployment rising from 4.1% to 4.4%. Stanford SIEPR notes the Fed faces a genuine stagflation dilemma: tariff-driven inflation says "hold" while weakening employment says "cut." Three FOMC members dissented at the December 2025 meeting — the most since 2019.
SIEPR Stanford / PIMCO / OMFIF Dec 2025 – Jan 2026
Fiscal Policy
One Big Beautiful Bill Act Adds AI & Tax Stimulus to Economy
Congress's July 2025 reconciliation bill provides $200 billion in household tax relief arriving as refund checks in early 2026. AI and tech investment, estimated to have contributed 0.5 points to 2025 GDP, is expected to continue. UCLA Anderson's March 2026 forecast shifted from "muddling through" to "reacceleration," citing converging fiscal stimulus and AI investment — though structural labor market imbalances persist.
UCLA Anderson / Deloitte / U.S. Chamber Mar 2026
Trade War
U.S.–China Truce After 9 Months of Escalation; Xi–Trump Summit in Busan
After a year of escalating reciprocal tariffs and export controls, Trump and Xi Jinping met in Busan in October 2025 to declare a truce. China meanwhile positioned itself as a "stable, pro-free trade alternative" to the U.S. amid tariff chaos. Critical minerals remain a core strategic pressure point — China's near-total control of many rare earths emerged as a primary geoeconomic vulnerability for the U.S.
Stimson Center / CFR Oct–Dec 2025

Topic Area 4
Healthcare & Entitlement Programs
3 stories
Medicaid
One Big Beautiful Bill Imposes Largest Medicaid Cut in History — 8.6M Lose Coverage
The Republican reconciliation legislation signed into law in July 2025 imposes approximately $700 billion in Medicaid cuts over 10 years, according to the CBO — the largest single-bill reduction in the program's history. The CBO estimates 8.6 million people will lose coverage. Key mechanisms include a new 80-hour monthly work requirement (taking effect in late 2026), more frequent eligibility verification, and tightened immigration status screening. The bill simultaneously triggers $45 billion in automatic Medicare sequestration cuts for 2026 under PAYGO, growing to $536 billion over nine years unless Congress acts to waive those cuts.

"Congressional Republicans cannot cut Medicaid by hundreds of billions without hurting people — and that is the point."

— Center on Budget and Policy Priorities, 2025
PBS NewsHour / CBO / CBPP Jul–Aug 2025
Long-Term Care
Nursing Home Staffing Standards at Risk; Medicaid Covers 6 in 10 Residents
The Biden administration's nursing home minimum staffing rules, set to phase in starting May 2026, are under threat from Republican rescission efforts. Medicaid covers more than 60% of nursing facility residents. If states cannot offset lost federal Medicaid funding, they face painful choices — cutting the program or slashing other budget areas like education.
CBPP / Medicare Rights Center 2025
HHS
RFK Jr. Faces Congressional Grilling as DOGE Dismantles HHS Programs
Health and Human Services Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. appeared before Senate Appropriations three times in a single week amid intense scrutiny of DOGE-led cuts to the department. Programs under scrutiny include the Ryan White AIDS program, cancer research funding, and vaccine advisory infrastructure. Kennedy's vaccine skepticism has become a flashpoint in hearings about the scope of HHS reform.
NBC News / PBS NewsHour May 2025

Education & Social Issues
3 stories
Immigration Policy
Mass Deportation Campaign Accelerates; 250,000 Asylum Seekers Face Expedited Removal
The administration is moving to dismiss asylum claims for an estimated 250,000+ individuals who entered unlawfully and have pending affirmative asylum applications, placing them in expedited removal without a full hearing. More than 1.45 million asylum applications were pending as of December 2024. The Alien Enemies Act has been invoked to deport suspected gang members without individual due process hearings, a policy that triggered emergency Supreme Court proceedings throughout 2025.
National Immigration Forum / NBC News Jun 2025
Civil Rights
Universal Injunctions Curtailed by Supreme Court; DEI Rollback Accelerates
The Supreme Court's 2024–2025 term sharply limited the ability of lower courts to issue nationwide injunctions blocking executive policies. The ruling has downstream effects on DEI regulations, environmental policies, and education guidance. Simultaneously, the Trump administration has aggressively rolled back DEI requirements across federal agencies and federal contractors, with executive orders targeting diversity programs at universities receiving federal funds.
Marshall Project / multiple outlets Jul 2025
Social Policy
Transgender Rights: Supreme Court Upholds Tennessee Ban on Gender-Affirming Care for Minors
The Supreme Court's 2025 ruling upholding Tennessee's ban on gender-affirming care for minors set a significant precedent that other states have moved to follow. The decision is among the most prominent social-issue rulings of the term and marks a significant shift from prior circuit court decisions that had blocked similar laws. Over 20 states had enacted or were considering comparable legislation by the time of the ruling.
PBS NewsHour 2025

Science, Technology & Environment
4 stories
Artificial Intelligence
DeepSeek Shock: Chinese AI Rivals U.S. Frontier Models at Fraction of Cost
In January 2025, Chinese startup DeepSeek released its R1 model — matching OpenAI's leading o1 reasoning model while reportedly costing under $6 million to train, compared to hundreds of billions spent by U.S. tech giants. The release wiped over $590 billion from Nvidia's market cap in a single day and became the most downloaded app in the United States within a week. Anthropic later alleged "industrial-scale" distillation campaigns — involving more than 150,000 exchanges — by DeepSeek and Chinese AI firms attempting to extract capabilities from its Claude model, citing major national security risks. Multiple states (Texas, New York, Virginia) banned the app from state devices. A bipartisan House bill (HR 1121) sought a federal ban on government devices.

"The panic isn't really about DeepSeek. It's about the realisation that AI capability is diffusing faster than anyone predicted."

— TechRound, Feb 2026
Stanford HAI / Atlantic Council / TechRound / Cornell J.L. Jan 2025 – Feb 2026
AI Governance
Congress Fails to Pass AI Regulation; Trump Orders DOJ to Sue States Over Laws
Congress passed no major AI regulation in 2025 beyond the TAKE IT DOWN Act (addressing nonconsensual intimate images). States passed bipartisan laws on deepfakes, algorithmic discrimination, and AI in healthcare. In response, tech companies poured hundreds of millions into super PACs. Trump issued an executive order directing the DOJ to challenge state AI consumer protection laws deemed "burdensome."
TechPolicy Press Jan 2026
Environment
One Big Beautiful Bill Eliminates Clean Energy Incentives; Climate Policy Retreats
The July 2025 reconciliation bill eliminated incentives for renewable energy investment, reversing key provisions of the Inflation Reduction Act. The administration also withdrew from the Paris Climate Accords. China, by contrast, accelerated domestic electrification investment and has positioned itself as the leading force in global clean energy adoption, according to CFR analysts.
CFR Climate Realism Initiative 2025
Critical Minerals
China's Critical Mineral Dominance Emerges as Core Geoeconomic Vulnerability
CFR's 2026 foreign policy outlook identified China's near-total control over critical minerals and rare earth elements as a top structural vulnerability for U.S. national security and manufacturing. Beijing demonstrated willingness to weaponize that control in 2025, sending a direct signal to U.S. and allied supply chains.
CFR Dec 2025

Business & U.S. Military
4 stories
Active Operations
Iran War Costs Surge Past $10B in Ten Days; Supplemental Funding Under Discussion
Operation Epic Fury has generated an estimated $10.35 billion in U.S. costs in its first ten days — roughly $1 billion per day — combining operational spending and equipment losses. Iran struck and damaged a U.S. early warning radar at Al-Udeid Air Base in Qatar (valued at $1.1 billion). Iran also targeted U.S. embassies and military installations across 9 Middle Eastern countries, as well as Strait of Hormuz shipping lanes through which 20% of the world's oil transits. The Penn Wharton Budget Model estimates a two-month campaign could cost $40–95 billion. Speaker Johnson has acknowledged supplemental funding discussions, noting a "cushion" of $153.3 billion in defense reconciliation funding. The total 2026 defense budget, after reconciliation additions, stands at $1 trillion — the first time in U.S. history.
Roll Call / CNN / Al Jazeera / Anadolu Agency Mar 2026
NDAA 2026
Defense Budget Hits $901B; Ukraine Aid Cut Sharply but Preserved
Trump signed the FY2026 NDAA in December 2025, authorizing $901 billion — $8 billion above the White House request. Ukraine Security Assistance Initiative funding was set at $400 million for 2026–2027, down from $14 billion in the April 2024 supplemental. The bill prohibits redirecting Ukraine-designated weapons into U.S. stocks without replacement. The NDAA also constrains Trump from drawing down U.S. troops in Europe below 76,000 without congressional notification.
OSW Centre for Eastern Studies / Atlantic Council Dec 2025
Munitions
Weapons Stockpile Depletion Raises Strategic Concerns Across Theaters
Military analysts warn that the Iran campaign is drawing down air defense interceptors (Patriot, THAAD, SM-6) that were originally designated for Ukraine and the Indo-Pacific. The Pentagon estimated 20,000 anti-drone interceptors were redirected from Ukraine to the Middle East in June 2025. Replacement manufacturing is slow — each interceptor takes months to produce. This creates a compounding strategic risk across simultaneous potential theaters.
Al Jazeera / CSIS Mar 2026
Tech & Business
Nvidia AI Chip Sales to China Allowed After December 2025 Reversal
Trump allowed Nvidia to sell H200 chips to China in December 2025, reversing a key export control restriction. The decision was part of a broader bilateral economic truce agreed at the October 2025 Busan summit. Critics argue the move undermines the goal of restricting China's AI military development while providing short-term revenue gains for U.S. chipmakers.
CFR Dec 2025
